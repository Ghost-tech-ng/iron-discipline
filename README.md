# Iron Discipline

**Personal fitness transformation OS — built for one user, built to last.**

Iron Discipline is a private mobile application for physique transformation. It centralises workout execution, macro tracking, supplement adherence, habit accountability, and progress analytics into a single disciplined system. No social feed, no gamification noise — just the tools that drive consistent results.

Built with React Native and Expo. Runs fully offline. Data stored on-device via SQLite — no server, no account, no breach risk.

---

## Philosophy

Most fitness apps are designed for mass-market retention, not personal transformation. Iron Discipline inverts that priority. Every screen is built around one outcome: making it harder to skip and easier to execute. The discipline score is not motivational — it is factual. It tells you exactly what percentage of your system you actually ran that day.

## Science-Based Nutrition Targets

All values are derived from peer-reviewed sports nutrition research (ISSN, Helms et al., Stokes et al.).

### Daily Macro Targets for 95 kg, 6'3" Recomposition

| Macro | Target | Rationale |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| **Protein** | **190–210 g/day** | 2.0–2.2 g/kg bodyweight — upper range recommended for fat loss phases to preserve lean mass (Helms et al., 2014; Phillips & Van Loon, 2011) |
| **Calories** | **2,400–2,600 kcal** | ~500 kcal below estimated TDEE (~2,950–3,100 for moderately active 95 kg male) — sufficient deficit for 0.5–0.75 kg fat loss/week |
| **Carbohydrates** | **220–260 g/day** | Performance fuel — prioritise around workouts |
| **Fats** | **65–80 g/day** | Hormonal baseline — don't go lower; testosterone drops below ~0.7 g/kg |

> **Why 190–210g protein?** At 95kg in a caloric deficit, protein synthesis rates drop. Hitting 2.0–2.2g/kg directly counteracts muscle breakdown, even while losing fat. This is the single most important target to hit daily.

### Meal Timing Protocol

| Meal | Timing | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| Breakfast | Within 60–90 min of waking | Break overnight fast, front-load protein |
| Pre-Workout | 60–90 min before training | Carb + protein — performance and muscle protection |
| Post-Workout | Within 45 min after training | Glycogen replenishment + muscle protein synthesis |
| Dinner | Evening — lower carb | Caloric control, not elimination |

### Optimal Meals — Researched Examples

**Chicken Salad (Dinner / Post-Workout)**

The ideal version for body recomposition:

| Ingredient | Quantity | Macros |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Grilled chicken breast | 200–250g | ~46–58g protein, ~220–275 kcal |
| Spinach + lettuce | 100g | 3g protein, ~20 kcal |
| Cucumber, tomatoes, onions, carrots | 200g | ~50 kcal |
| Olive oil | 1 tbsp | 14g fat, 120 kcal |
| Lemon juice | Freely | ~0 kcal |

Total: **~410–465 kcal, ~49–61g protein** — excellent macro density.

**What ruins chicken salad:**
- Mayonnaise (100 kcal/tbsp, minimal nutrition) → replace with Greek yogurt dressing
- Creamy dressings → can add 300–500 kcal invisibly
- Fried chicken → doubles the calorie count
- Sugary sauces → spike insulin, add empty calories

**Carb addition rule:** If eaten pre- or post-workout, add 150g cooked sweet potato or 100g rice (~170–180 kcal, 35–40g carbs) for better performance and recovery. Skip the added carbs if it's a rest day evening meal.

**Other high-value meals for this goal:**
- Eggs (3–4 whole + 3 whites) + oats + banana — ~500 kcal, 40g protein — ideal breakfast
- Greek yogurt (200g) + berries + whey scoop — 350 kcal, 45g protein — snack or second breakfast
- Rice (150g cooked) + lean beef (200g) + broccoli — ~550 kcal, 48g protein — lunch or post-workout
- Oats (100g dry) + whey + peanut butter (15g) — pre-workout meal

## Workout System — Evidence-Based 6-Day PPL Split

**Program:** Push / Pull / Legs (PPL) — repeated twice per week
**Research basis:** PPL at 2× frequency produces superior hypertrophy vs 1× frequency splits (Schoenfeld et al., 2016; meta-analysis of 10 studies). This is the most validated natural bodybuilding split for intermediate lifters.

### Weekly Timetable — 5-Day Split

**Format:** Push / Pull / Legs / Upper / Lower
Every muscle group is trained **twice per week** — same frequency benefit as 6-day PPL, one fewer session.

| Day | Session | Muscle Groups |
|-----|---------|--------------|
| Monday | Push | Chest (primary), Shoulders, Triceps |
| Tuesday | Pull | Back width + thickness, Biceps |
| Wednesday | Legs | Quadriceps (primary), Hamstrings, Calves, Core |
| Thursday | **Rest** | — |
| Friday | Upper | Chest + Back + Shoulders + Arms (compound re-stimulus) |
| Saturday | Lower | Hamstrings + Glutes (primary), Quads, Calves, Core |
| Sunday | Rest | 30-min walk — active recovery only |

> **Why this works:** Research on training frequency (Schoenfeld et al., 2016) shows 2×/week per muscle group produces 3.1% more hypertrophy than 1×/week. This 5-day structure preserves that advantage — chest is hit Mon + Fri, back Tue + Fri, legs Wed + Sat, arms Tue + Fri. Thursday rest is strategically placed mid-week for recovery before the Friday/Saturday double.

### Cardio Protocol

| Type | Frequency | Duration | Timing | Purpose |
|------|-----------|----------|--------|---------|
| LISS (brisk walk / light bike) | 3–4×/week | 25–35 min | Post-workout or morning | Fat oxidation without cortisol spike |
| HIIT (sprint intervals) | 1–2×/week | 15–20 min | Only on non-leg days | Metabolic conditioning |
| Daily steps | 7 days | 8,000–10,000 steps | Throughout day | NEAT — underrated fat loss tool |

> Research note: HIIT is not superior to LISS for fat loss when total energy expenditure is matched. LISS is better tolerated on high-volume training days and preserves more recovery capacity (Wilson et al., 2012).

### Progressive Overload Rules

The app enforces these automatically — previous session weight is shown at every set:

1. When you complete all sets at the top of the rep range → add weight next session
2. Compounds (squat, deadlift, bench, OHP): add 2.5kg
3. Isolation and machine: add 2.5kg or go one rep higher
4. Never sacrifice form for weight — form is the rep

## Weekly Progress Tracking Protocol

### Weigh-In Rules (Critical — Do Not Skip)

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| Same day every week (Monday morning) | Eliminates day-to-day variation |
| First thing after waking, after toilet, before food or water | Lowest body water state — most consistent reading |
| Same scale, same surface | Scale variance can be ±0.5 kg between surfaces |
| Log even bad weeks | Trend matters, not individual readings |
| Judge over 2–3 weeks, not 1 week | Water weight fluctuates ±1.5–2 kg daily |

### Expected Progress Timeline (Realistic, Research-Based)

| Week | Expected Weight Change | What's Happening |
|------|----------------------|-----------------|
| 1–2 | −1.5 to −3 kg | Mostly water + glycogen depletion — looks fast, don't get overconfident |
| 3–6 | −0.5 to −0.75 kg/week | True fat loss begins |
| 7–10 | −0.4 to −0.6 kg/week | Rate slows slightly — this is normal, do not cut calories aggressively |
| 11–12 | −0.3 to −0.5 kg/week | Final phase — hold form and intensity |

**3-month target from 95 kg:** ~88–91 kg (4–7 kg true fat loss) while maintaining or gaining lean mass.
This is a visible, significant change at 6'3" — leaner face, visible shoulder separation, flatter stomach.

### Body Measurements to Track (Beyond Scale Weight)

| Measurement | How Often | Tool |
|-------------|-----------|------|
| Bodyweight | Weekly (Monday AM) | Scale |
| Waist circumference | Every 2 weeks | Tape measure, at navel |
| Progress photos | Every 2 weeks | Same lighting, same pose, same time |
| Bench press 1RM estimate | Monthly | Strength = muscle retention indicator |
| Squat 1RM estimate | Monthly | Leg strength indicator |

If weight stalls but waist is dropping → good. If both stall → evaluate calories.

## Features

### Dashboard — Command Center

The first screen you see every morning.

- **Daily Readiness Card** — today's workout, calories remaining, protein remaining, water intake, checklist completion, current streak
- **Discipline Score** — a single percentage computed from every tracked variable for the day. No rounding up.
- **Quick Actions** — log a meal, start today's workout, mark supplements taken
- **Weekly weigh-in prompt** — appears every Monday morning with last week's weight shown

### Workout System

- Weekly PPL split — pre-configured with the full 6-day timetable above
- Exercise cards with sets, reps, weight, rest timer, and notes per set
- Previous session weight + volume shown inline while logging (progressive overload enforcement)
- Workout completion triggers streak update, estimated calorie burn, and discipline score contribution

### Nutrition Tracking

- Daily macro targets: calories, protein, carbs, fats — pre-set to profile above
- Meal categories: breakfast, lunch, dinner, post-workout, snacks
- Smart progress bars showing current vs. target in real time
- Common food library pre-loaded with the researched meals above
- Barcode scanner for packaged foods (Phase 2)

### Supplement Tracking

- Pre-configured for the stack above (creatine, whey, omega-3, Vitamin D3, magnesium, minoxidil)
- Push notification reminders per supplement (time-configurable)
- One-tap mark-as-taken

### Habit Tracking

- Daily checklist: 10k steps, 8h sleep, no junk food, water goal, cardio
- Checkbox UI with haptic feedback and completion animations
- Missed habits feed directly into discipline score reduction

### Progress Tracking

- Weekly body weight + waist logging (Monday prompt)
- Progress photo upload with side-by-side comparison view
- Charts: weight trend, workout consistency, calorie adherence, strength trend, streak history
- Discipline score history graph
- Projected trend line toward 12-week goal weight

### Discipline System

Every tracked variable has a weighted contribution to a daily discipline percentage:

| Variable | Weight |
|----------|--------|
| Workout completed | 25% |
| Protein target hit | 20% |
| Calorie target hit | 15% |
| Supplements taken | 15% |
| Water goal | 10% |
| Sleep logged | 10% |
| Cardio / steps | 5% |

Streaks tracked separately: workout streak, nutrition streak, full discipline streak.

### Notifications

- Supplement reminders at configured times
- Workout reminder if none logged by set hour
- Protein and calorie warnings at end of day
- Streak break alert
- Monday morning weigh-in reminder (7:00 AM)
